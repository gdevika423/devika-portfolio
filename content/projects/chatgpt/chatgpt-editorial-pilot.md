## Overview
In response to growing bottlenecks in Zeta's documentation development lifecycle (DDLC), I initiated a pilot project to explore the use of GenAI (ChatGPT) for accelerating editorial reviews across technical communications.

## Problem
Traditional document review processes faced delays due to a shortage of editorial bandwidth, resulting in slower sprint delivery cycles and inconsistent adherence to style guides.

## Solution
I designed a controlled pilot to evaluate ChatGPT’s capabilities, using a two-stage editorial prompt system:

- **Stage 1:** Language review for clarity, consistency, and tone alignment.
- **Stage 2:** Format validation against Zeta’s documentation standards.

A structured prompt framework was created and tested across multiple document types, including feature guides, integration manuals, and API documentation.

## Outcome
- Achieved **30–40% faster editorial cycles** compared to traditional manual reviews.
- Successfully standardized document tone and structure across sprints.
- Reduced manual dependency without compromising quality.
- Provided a scalable foundation for future editorial automation at Zeta.

## Key Contributions
- Led the pilot initiative end-to-end, from problem identification to solution rollout.
- Collaborated cross-functionally with technical writers, product managers, and engineering teams to validate outputs.
- Created detailed editorial prompt templates adaptable for different document types.

## Lessons Learned
While GenAI proved highly effective for standardizing language and format, human editorial judgment remains essential for deep technical validation.  
This pilot confirmed that **AI + Human Collaboration** delivers the best outcomes in technical communications.
